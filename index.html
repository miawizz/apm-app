Playful Strategies

Tap a strategy for a reminder and the example video

Give objects or body parts a personality. Let the spoon beg not to be eaten. Let the backpack complain it’s too full.

Act like a robot, a machine, or use sound effects. Think vacuum arms or beep-boop toothbrush mode.

Use reverse psychology playfully. “Don’t eat that broccoli! It’s mine!”

Turn anything into a challenge. Can you put your shoes on before I count to 10?

Leave clues. Whisper to a pretend wizard. Act confused by how shoes got on the ceiling.

Singing turns tension into silliness. Narrate your next move with dramatic flair.

Become a waiter, wizard, detective, or sleepy sloth. Invite your child to play along.

Tell stories to teach, reflect, or connect. Use your voice and imagination as the tool.

Make the ordinary feel special. Pretend it’s a big deal to wash hands or check the mail.

Use movement or a deep breath to reset, regulate, or redirect.

Embrace the ridiculous. Gibberish, sound effects, or fake falling asleep all count.
